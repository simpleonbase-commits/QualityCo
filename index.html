Mission

Start with one hard biological question. Build a repeatable research engine.

Quality Company exists to dissolve quality-of-life problems through organized research, public data, and community-supported compute. Cancer protein-complex research is the first cell in a longer line of problems we intend to investigate with discipline, humility, and transparent evidence standards.

The immediate mission is to study whether pathological S100A10/p11 activity, especially through the annexin A2 complex, can be selectively disrupted in cancer cells to reduce invasion, metastasis, or survival signaling in specific contexts.

Research pillars

A lean research cell before a lab.

This website frames the work as literature review, public data analysis, hypothesis development, and target validation planning only.

01

Literature review

Build a structured evidence base around S100A10/p11, ANXA2, the ANXA2-S100A10 complex, plasmin generation, invasion, metastasis, apoptosis resistance, and tumor immune context.

02

Public dataset analysis

Use public resources such as TCGA, DepMap, CCLE, CPTAC, GEO, Human Protein Atlas, cBioPortal, NCBI Gene, and PubMed before considering any experimental work.

03

Mechanism mapping

Map ANXA2 binding, plasminogen binding, plasmin generation, extracellular matrix remodeling, MMP activation, immune remodeling, migration, invasion, and drug-resistance hypotheses.

04

Cancer-type prioritization

Compare cancer contexts by human tumor expression, survival association, functional evidence, ANXA2/S100A10 mechanism evidence, targetability, data availability, and therapeutic gap.

Mechanism anchor

ANXA2 appears to organize S100A10 at membranes.

Reviews describe S100A10/p11 as commonly paired with annexin A2 in the AIIt heterotetramer, a setup that can place S100A10 at membrane surfaces where cancer-relevant protease biology may occur.

Evidence maturity
Kwon et al., 2005 — plasminogen receptor review Madureira et al., 2016 — RAS transformation and S100A10 Phipps et al., 2011 — tumor-promoting macrophage migration Shang et al., 2013 — colorectal cancer biomarker study Bharadwaj et al., 2021 — ANXA2/S100A10 complex review

Hypothesis ladder

Every claim must earn its way up the ladder.

  1. S100A10/p11 is overexpressed in specific cancer types compared with normal tissue.
  2. High S100A10 expression correlates with worse outcomes such as metastasis, recurrence, resistance, or reduced survival.
  3. S100A10 functionally contributes to invasion, migration, plasmin generation, matrix degradation, angiogenesis, apoptosis resistance, or immune remodeling.
  4. S100A10 works partly through ANXA2 and the ANXA2-S100A10 complex.
  5. Disrupting expression, localization, or ANXA2 interaction reduces cancer-relevant behavior in specific models.
  6. Any future strategy would require tumor-specific targeting to avoid broad disruption of normal S100A10 functions.

Safety boundary

Research organization only.

This initiative does not provide clinical advice, treatment recommendations, self-experimentation guidance, or wet-lab instructions. Any future biological experiments must be performed by qualified professionals in approved institutions or contract research organizations under applicable safety, ethics, and regulatory oversight.

  • No wet-lab experiments
  • No cell culture or human sample handling
  • No animal studies or genetic modification
  • No viral vector work
  • No patient-specific medical guidance
  • No treatment or cure claims
